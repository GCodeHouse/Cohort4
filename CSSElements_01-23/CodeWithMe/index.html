Katherine Johnson

The human computer and a hidden figure

Here's a time line of Katherine Johsnon's life:

  • 1928 - Katherine finishes eighth grade at age 10
  • 1936 - Katherine graduates college at 18
  • 1937 - Katherine Johnson graduated from West Virginia State College with a bachelor’s degree in mathematics and French
  • 1939 - Katherine Johnson married James Francis Goble
  • 1953 - Katherine Johnson began working with the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics as a “computer” doing complex analysis and calculations
  • 1956 - Katherine Johnson's first husband died of a brain tumor
  • 1958 - Katherine gets assigned the first man space mission
  • 1959 - Katherine Johnson married Lt. Colonel James A. Johnson
  • 1960 - She co-authored a paper on calculations for placing a spacecraft into orbit.
  • 1961 - Katherine Johnson's work was crucial in calculating the trajectory for Alan Shepard’s historic suborbital flight -- Freedom 7.
  • 1962 - John Glenn became the first American to orbit Earth. By that time, NASA had begun to use electronic computers. However, before Glenn left the ground, he wanted to make sure that the electronic computer had planned his flight correctly. He asked to have Johnson double check the computer’s calculation
  • 1967 - Katherine wins the NASA Lunar Orbiter Spacecraft and Operations team award
  • 1969 - Katherine was also part of the team that calculated where and when to launch the rocket for the Apollo 11 mission of 1969, which sent the first three men to the Moon.
  • 1986 - She retired from NASA.
  • 2015 - Katherine Johnson received the Presidential Medal of Freedom from President Obama for her accomplishments in the field of mathematics and science.
  • 2016 - NASA dedicated the 40,000 square foot Katherine G. Johnson Computational Research Facility. Also in 2016, the book Hidden Figures: The American Dream and the Untold Story of the Black Women Mathematicians Who Helped Win the Space Race. The movie was also released.
  • 2018 - West Virginia State University unveiled a statue of Katherine Johnson on campus and dedicated a scholarship in her honor.
  • 2020 - Katherine Johnson died in Hampton, Virginia at age 101.
